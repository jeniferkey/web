10/12/03
Bob's First Cross Country School
at Quail Run
We had a great day. The event we planned to go to was cancelled due to rain, so
we quickly changed our plans and just went out to school at a different farm.
I was nervous for our first time out, but Bob was wonderful. Our first warm up fence was an X,
and Bob made it obvious that he wasn't sure why we were there. We trotted up to it, and he stopped and
walked over it. A few more times around and we were cantering away and ready for the
vertical and oxer. The only stop we had out on the course was a drop jump that had a ditch
in it. We trotted up to it happily, and right at the take-off he spotted the ditch and had to take a look.
The next two times he hesitated a little, but jumped to the other side.
 
 
We were a little sloppy starting out. This was his first solid fence, and he wasn't sure if I really wanted him to go over it.
 
He soon figured it out.
The drop jump with the ditch. I just grabbed the saddle and gave him his head.
 
We worked a lot on cantering away..
He went into the water a little sideways the first time, but figured out trotting through, and....
 
Jumping out. He was very willing about it all. He did have a habit of jumping into the water, which was a bit of a surprise.
We cantered away and jumped up the bank.
 
First try over the bigger ditch. No trouble.
More cantering away practice.
 
He really gives the feeling of power.
It was just a rub, but he did scare himself.
 
So, he made sure he wouldn't touch it again.
Every jump after the rub he was a powerhouse. He didn't rush, or act worried, but he'd bascule and have room to spare.
 
He was just perfect for our last fence. We cantered to it out of the woods, cantered on landing, and you can see he did a great job of the actual jump...
