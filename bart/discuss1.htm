A well-constructed argument meets four criteria:

Relevance: Its individual assertions are relevant to the subject under discussion.
Acceptibility: Its assertions are acceptable to those involved in the discussion.
Sufficiency: Its assertions are together sufficient to support the stated conclusion.
Rebuttal: It directly addresses the strongest counterarguments.
