1640. Cynedda "The Great"50 WELEDIG(1278) birth date unknown. Cynedda died after 400.

He married unknown. Cynedda (Cunedda) was thought by Nennius to have come to Gwynedd from the "Men of the North"--what today is southern Scotland. The theory is that Cunedda was of the tribe of the Votadini, and that numbers of these migrated into northern Wales (pushing out the proto-Irish) between A.D. 380 and 400 when Magnus Maximus was pulling out the legions. (The garrison at Segontium--latter-day Caernarvon--was stripped in 383.) There is even the claim that Cunedda's grandfather was named Padarn Beisrudd, or "Paturnus of the red cloak," suggesting the red cloak of a Roman officer.
